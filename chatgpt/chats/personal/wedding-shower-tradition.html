What is the history of why we have wedding showers for the bride?

Wedding showers for the bride have a long history that dates back to the late 16th century. It is believed that the tradition originated in the Netherlands and was brought to America by Dutch settlers. At that time, brides-to-be would have a "kitchen shower" where friends and family members would bring practical household items to help set up the couple's home.

In the 19th century, the tradition of bridal showers evolved into more of a social event. Women would gather to spend time with the bride, share advice and well wishes, and give gifts. The gifts were typically things that the bride would need for her married life, such as linens, appliances, and other household items.

Over the years, bridal showers have continued to evolve and become more elaborate and often include games, refreshments, and other festivities. Despite the changes, the underlying theme of wishing the bride well and helping her get ready for her married life has remained the same.

The term "shower" in the context of a bridal shower is thought to refer to the showering of gifts upon the bride. The idea is that the bride is "showered" with gifts, much like she would be showered with water in a literal sense. The term "shower" conveys the idea of a wealth or abundance of gifts and well wishes, and it has been used in this context for many decades.
